FAQ

What is TradeSparrow?
We provide web tools to perform technical analysis on stocks/equities. Our tools are efficient and super easy to use so that newbies and casual traders/investors can jump in right away.
What is technical trading?
This is a method of trading where buying and selling decisions can be reasoned as follows: "I've seen this kind of price movement pattern several times before and 9 out of 10 times it moves higher over the next hour/day/week/month. There is a good chance that I can profit if I enter." A technical trader identifies repeating patterns and acts on the knowledge that the patterns indicate a higher probability of one outcome over another.
If this works, then why does it work? Do such patterns really exist?
We are assuming you're a small investor ($10k - $50k). Large investors (100's of $millions) have an overwhelming effect in which direction a stock goes. When they decide to enter (or exit), they leave behind traces. If you can pick up on these traces or patterns, then you can ride the wave on the back of the whale, as they say.
Okay I get it, but can I really compete against those guys, the guys with the super computers and PhD programmers?
You (the small guy) are a minnow. You can move quick, increase or reduce your total risk profile at a drop of the hat. Big players with $100's millions or $billions cannot. They have to use every speed trick, whether it's faster programs or faster computers, just to make their whale look and act like a minnow. They have to be able move their money without drawing attention or making huge waves. They can try, but you have the innate advantage of being small.
My financial adviser and mainstream opinion say I cannot beat the market by trading it.
Tell it to Goldman Sachs. Here is the fact by the numbers: If you bought a stock at any given day at the closing price with the intent to sell it at the close within the next 30 days, what is the probability that there will be an opportunity given to you to sell this stock at a profit? The answer is 29/30, or a 96.7% chance of winning. There is only one day out of the total 30 days that you could have bought at the absolute highest price and hence a 1/30 (3.3%) chance that makes this trade a definite loser from the start. Despite the odds, most traders will not be able to turn a profit. When the stock moves in the trader's favor, he holds (greed). When the price turns around and erases all his gain, which it likely will as there is also only a 3.3% chance that he bought at the perfectly lowest price, then he sells out of fear. Rinse and repeat. By using technical signals trader can use quantitative data (statistics) to govern his buying and selling decisions rather than raw emotions.

Why are you doing this?

On September 14th 2010, a AP-CNBC poll revealed that: "the perception that the market is unfair is widespread. Nearly 90 percent of those with portfolios of less than $50,000 said the market is unfair to small investors. ...More than 75 percent of investors worth at least $250,000 say the market is unfair to the little guy." Due to certain artificial economic incentives, huge sums money have been shifted out of Main Street and reallocated into Wall Street mega funds in a bid to seek higher returns trading against the little guy. To whatever extent we can do, we would like to see the trend reverse by helping the little guy play better.
